About Tim Berners-Lee

Early life and education.

  • Tim was born in london, England in 1955.
  • His parents were Mary Lee-woods and Conway Berners-Lee.
  • HIs parents worked on the first commercially-built computer, the Ferranti Mark Ⅰ.
  • His primary education from Sheen Mount Primary School and London's independent Emanuel School.
  • He studies at The Queen's College where he received his first-class degree in Physics in 1973.

How he came to work at CERN.

Tim Berners-Lee joined CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research, in 1980 as an independent contractor. At CERN, he was tasked with helping manage the vast amount of data generated by the organization’s research.

During this time, he developed a system called Enquire, which allowed scientists to share and retrieve information efficiently. Although he left CERN briefly, Berners-Lee returned in 1984 as a full-time employee.

By then, CERN had become the largest internet node in Europe. Recognizing the challenges of sharing research and collaborating across a global network of scientists, Berners-Lee envisioned a system to simplify communication.

This vision led to the development of the World Wide Web in 1989, based on concepts he had first explored with Enquire. In summary, his work at CERN provided both the inspiration and the practical environment to create the groundbreaking technologies that underpin the modern web.

The Proposal for the World Wide Web (1989):

  • Berners-Lee wrote a proposal in March 1989 titled "Information Management: A Proposal", outlining a system for sharing and accessing information globally.
  • His supervisor, Mike Sendall, famously described it as "vague but exciting."

Development of the Web Technologies (1990):

  • By 1990, Berners-Lee had developed the key technologies for the World Wide Web:
  • HTML (HyperText Markup Language): To structure and display content.
  • HTTP (HyperText Transfer Protocol): For communication between clients and servers.
  • URL (Uniform Resource Locator): To uniquely identify resources on the web.
  • Created the first web browser (WorldWideWeb) and first web server (httpd).
